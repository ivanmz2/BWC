See how it works

Both black coffee and espresso are made by steeping beans with hot water, so what's the difference between the two? As explained above, espresso is made of finely ground, extra-dark beans, that are run through a pressurized machine that produces only one, highly-concentrated shot at a time. Black coffee is more simple and humble: hot water dripped over ground coffee beans for a low-to medium-concentration of coffee and caffeine.
